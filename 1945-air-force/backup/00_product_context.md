# 0. Product Context

## 0.1 Game Summary

1945 Air Force is a mobile vertical shoot 'em up where the player drags a plane with one finger, dodges bullets/enemies, auto-shoots continuously, and clears short wave/boss stages. Its core fantasy is an arcade WWII power fantasy: pilot a warplane, upgrade firepower, wipe the screen, and gradually unlock more support systems.

The initial appeal comes from fast entry into action, easy controls, constant shooting/explosion feedback, and visible progression after a few runs. It evokes the familiar feel of classic arcade shoot 'em ups while layering modern F2P progression on top.

## 0.3 Design Thesis

1945 Air Force has sustained long-term retention by combining a very simple action core with a layered metagame. The combat asks for one primary skill: move well enough to survive while auto-fire handles offense. The business and retention depth comes from everything around that combat: aircraft collection, wingman/device setup, upgrades, campaign gates, PvP-like score competition, daily missions, quests, events, VIP, ads, and IAP.

1945 uses the following F2P retention pattern:

Simple Control (drag movement, auto-shoot) → Short Battle (short stages, easy to replay) → Run Reward (gold/gems/modules/quest progress) → Power Investment (upgrade aircraft/wingman/device) → Harder Content (harder levels, bosses, new modes) → Return Hooks (daily gifts, quests, events, PvP attempts).
